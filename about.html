Our story

A small studio with old standards

Sakina Studio is a small digital studio making Islamic printables for everyday deen — Qur'an memorization and prayer trackers, Arabic learning for children, geometric art for quiet walls, and companions for the Ramadan season.

Everything is designed in-house on a foundation of classical Islamic geometry and open-licensed calligraphic type, then produced with the same care you'd want in a mushaf margin: verified data, print-true sizing, and no shortcuts.

We're a young studio with old standards. If a page isn't something we'd hang in our own home or hand to our own children, it doesn't ship.

What "sakina" means

Sakīnah (سكينة) is tranquility — the settled calm of a heart at rest. It's the quality we try to put into every page: soft palettes, honest materials, geometry with centuries of patience in it, and typography that never shouts.

Five promises

  • Qur'an-related data verified against Tanzil — never typed from memory
  • No ayah text on pages meant to be printed and discarded
  • Hyperlinked, bookmarked PDFs — planners that navigate like apps
  • Ink-friendly by construction: white backgrounds, thin lines, calm accents
  • Multiple paper sizes always: US Letter, A4, A5 and beyond
